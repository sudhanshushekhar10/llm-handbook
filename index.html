How to read this guide
01 · ORIENTATION

Start with analogies

Every concept is mapped to something you already know — a lexer, a cache, a git patch, a load balancer. The ML word comes second.

02 · DEPTH ON DEMAND

Skim, then dive

Read the narrative top-to-bottom; open the deep-dive panels only when you want the math. Hover any dotted term for a definition.

03 · HANDS-ON

Run the code

Tutorials ship with a Local (Mac/MPS) tab and a Cloud (CUDA) tab. Copy, run, break, learn.

04 · RECEIPTS

Follow the citations

Numbered chips like link to the original paper. The field is young enough to read the sources.

About the Author

Who wrote this

Sudhanshu Shekhar is an engineering leader who builds and runs large-scale, product-driven systems used by millions — where software architecture, the hard edges of distributed systems, and increasingly AI-native engineering all have to hold together in production. He works deep across the stack, from system design and architecture to bringing AI into real products. He also holds two patents.

Mentoring and coaching engineers is the part of the work he cares about most, and this field guide grew out of the same instinct — a desire to give back to the community, and to hand fellow engineers the map he wishes he'd had. He authored it for the engineer he used to be: someone who could already ship the API, the database, and the distributed system, but wanted to actually understand the model now sitting inside all of them.

The views expressed here are his own.  ·  Connect on LinkedIn →
